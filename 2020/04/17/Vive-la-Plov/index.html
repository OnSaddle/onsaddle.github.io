Vive la Plov! 抓飯萬歲!

Author: Wolf Date: April 17, 2020  16:06:24 Category: WhatsCooking

My answer to the current unpleasant xenophobic sentiment can be found in food.

Using affordable, local ingredients I always like to try many cuisines from other cultures. The more you come to understand the history of the world, the less likely you will consider the Chinese culture a monotonous, closed and isolated existence.

In history the Chinese as a people has never closed the door towards dietary interaction with other parts of the world. Why let conservatism and nationalism blind us now?

Since my travel to Xinjiang in 2014 I have been really into plov 抓飯, the signature food of central Asia. In the old quarters of Urumqi, there are many small Uyghur restaurants specialised in plov (they call it “polo” while the Han call it “抓飯” because traditionally it is eaten with your fingers).

This restaurant’s specialty is “plov in small-pot” 小鍋抓飯. Plov, as a traditional festive food, is usually cooked in a huge wok to feed a large number of guests. Now that plov has become more like a daily treat for everyone visiting the region, there’s a new trend to cook it in relatively small pots to allow more flexibility.

It’s a hearty, nutritious and very flavourful dish. I tried to make it at home for a few times, and it proves to be quite newbie-friendly :-)
